The technique

The technique that reaches where nothing else can.

Standard facial massage works on the surface. It softens what it can reach — but the muscles that hold the most tension aren't reachable from the outside. The masseter at the back of the jaw. The pterygoids deep behind the cheekbone. The buccinator that shapes the inner cheek. These are the muscles that carry years of clenching, grinding, and stored stress.

Buccal massage — pronounced buckle — is an intraoral technique. Using gloved hands, Tenielle works from inside the mouth to release these structures directly: where the tension actually lives, where the muscle actually begins, where real change is possible.

Masseter

The primary jaw muscle — the main driver of clenching, grinding, and TMJ pain. Often visibly enlarged in habitual grinders. Releases directly with intraoral technique.

Pterygoids

Deep muscles behind the cheekbone that control jaw movement and restrict opening. Unreachable from outside. Intraoral release reduces clicking and restricted jaw motion.

Buccinator

The muscle that shapes the inner cheek and defines its contour. Releasing it lifts the midface and softens muscle-related hollowing and asymmetry.

Fascia

The connective tissue that holds the whole face in its habitual shape. Myofascial release softens chronic holding patterns — the face restructures over time.

Your session

What to expect at Soulful Sculpt.

Sessions run 60 minutes at the Ashmore studio. Sterile single-use gloves throughout. A protocol built around your specific tension patterns, skin, and goals.

01

Arrival & Consultation

Your session begins with a brief consultation. Tenielle assesses where you hold tension in the jaw, face, and neck — and understands your specific goals — before a single technique is applied.

02

External Preparation

Neck, scalp, and facial massage relaxes the superficial layers and warms the deeper muscles. This also allows Tenielle to read how the jaw is holding before moving intraorally.

Using sterile gloved hands, Tenielle works from inside the mouth to release the masseter, pterygoid, and buccal muscles at their deepest layers — the part of the session no external technique can replicate.

Lifting strokes, gua sha, and manual lymphatic drainage work together to de-puff, contour, and define the face. Visible results from this sequence are usually immediate.

Aftercare & Guidance

A hydrating finish and personalised aftercare guidance to support your results between sessions. Tenielle will recommend a schedule based on your specific tension and aesthetic goals.

The Nervous System Element

Soulful Sculpt's approach addresses more than the physical. The face stores emotional and neurological tension — and Tenielle's technique creates the safety for that deeper layer to release as well. Clients often feel an unexpected softening alongside the physical change.

Buccal massage (pronounced buckle) is an intraoral facial massage technique. Using gloved hands, the therapist works from inside the mouth to release the deep facial and jaw muscles — including the masseter, pterygoids, and buccinator — that are impossible to reach effectively from the outside. The treatment addresses both jaw tension and aesthetic concerns including loss of definition, puffiness, and muscle-related ageing.

There is some pressure sensation inside the mouth, particularly if you carry significant jaw tension. Most clients describe it as intense but deeply releasing — similar to a deep tissue massage on a tight muscle. Discomfort typically reduces noticeably with each session as chronic tension is addressed. Tenielle works at a pace and pressure that suits each individual client.

Most clients notice visible lifting and relief after their very first session. For lasting structural change — softening chronic jaw tension and reshaping muscle definition — a course of sessions is recommended. Most clients start with 4–6 sessions and then maintain every 4–6 weeks depending on how much tension they carry and their aesthetic goals.
